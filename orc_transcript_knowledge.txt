ORACLE RECRUITING CLOUD - KNOWLEDGE BASE

=== RECRUITING TYPES ===

RULE: Use "Professional" recruiting type when hiring in countries where InfoBlocks has operations (primarily US and India).

RULE: Use "FT Contractor" recruiting type when hiring in countries where InfoBlocks does NOT have operations but does business on a conditional basis. Examples include Norway and Switzerland.

RULE: Use "Confidential" recruiting type for senior and executive roles such as CFO, Chief People Officer, or other senior leadership positions.

RULE: Use "Apprentice" recruiting type specifically for India interns. In India, interns are called apprentices, not interns.

RULE: Use "Contingent" recruiting type for contractors.

RULE: In production, the "Use" field should always be set to "Job". No other options are used in production.

=== REQUISITION TYPES ===

RULE: Use "Standard" requisition type for immediate hiring needs. Standard requisitions can be posted to job boards.

RULE: Use "Pipeline" requisition type for future bulk hiring needs, such as forecasting that you need to hire 15 interns. Pipeline requisitions remain open for ongoing candidate collection and do not have a Target Hire Date.

RULE: Always set Number of Openings to "Limited". Never select "Unlimited".

=== HIRING TEAM RULES ===

RULE: Individual contributors cannot be added as hiring managers. The system will give a warning if you try to select an inappropriate person as a hiring manager.

RULE: Recruiter role can be given to anyone.

RULE: Hiring team roles include: Recruiter, Additional Hiring Manager, and Scheduler.

RULE: The Scheduler is responsible for managing the interview process and onboarding with the TA OPS team.

RULE: Anyone added to the hiring team will have access to that requisition.

RULE: In practice, hiring managers do not create requisitions themselves. They tell the TA OPS team what opening they need, and TA OPS creates the requisition.

=== BUSINESS UNIT AND LOCATION RULES ===

RULE: Select the Business Unit based on where you are hiring. Use India Business Unit for India hires, US Business Unit for US hires.

RULE: The Legal Employer defaults automatically based on the Business Unit selected. US Business Unit defaults to InfoBlocks Inc. India Business Unit defaults to InfoBlocks Technical Support.

RULE: Currency defaults automatically based on the Business Unit selected.

RULE: Primary Location can be different from the Business Unit's standard geography.

RULE: Multiple additional locations can be added to a requisition.

RULE: If a job does not already exist in the system, you must create the job first before it will appear in the list of values for selection.

=== CANDIDATE SELECTION PROCESS (CSP) ===

RULE: The Candidate Selection Process (CSP) defines the states and steps a candidate goes through during recruitment.

RULE: Different CSP flows exist for different hiring types. For example, intern/apprentice roles may only need one interview round, while professional roles may have multiple rounds.

RULE: There are two main CSP flows: one for US candidates and one for non-US candidates. The US flow includes additional compliance questions around disability, gender, and race that are not required for other countries.

=== JOB POSTING RULES ===

RULE: A job can be posted to internal career site only, external career site only, or both.

RULE: You can schedule internal posting first, then delay external posting. For example, post internally for 15 days to see if internal candidates are interested before posting externally.

RULE: Internal employees can find posted jobs under "Opportunity Marketplace" in the Me section.

RULE: External candidates apply through the external career site.

RULE: The Requisition Title is what candidates see on job boards like LinkedIn. It should be written in plain language, not internal job codes.

=== BACKGROUND CHECK AND ASSESSMENTS ===

RULE: InfoBlocks uses a third party provider for background checks. There are two accounts: one for US candidates and one for non-US candidates.

RULE: Background check packages must be selected when setting up the requisition. US positions use the United States package.

RULE: Assessments can also be configured at the requisition level.

=== APPROVAL WORKFLOW ===

RULE: After submission, a requisition goes to "Pending Approval" status before becoming "Open".

RULE: Approvers are notified when a requisition is submitted for approval.

RULE: In lower/development environments, approval may be bypassed and the requisition automatically approved.

RULE: Once approved, the requisition status changes to "Open" and can then be posted.

=== POSTING DESCRIPTIONS ===

RULE: Separate posting descriptions can be written for internal and external candidates.

RULE: Attachments such as company benefits information can be added to the job posting.

=== SALARY AND COMPENSATION ===

RULE: Compensation currency is automatically set based on the Business Unit selected.

RULE: In production, the system displays minimum, mid, and maximum salary ranges based on location and job type.

RULE: The HEPA position field applies only to US employees and should be selected yes by default for US job requisitions.

RULE: Sales Mix and Commission fields are only applicable for sales roles.

RULE: Corporate bonus percentage is defaulted from the job level and does not need to be manually entered.
